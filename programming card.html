Sometimes, even creativity needs a break. When I feel boxed in by design rules and aesthetics, I turn to programming—a place where logic and structure bring a different kind of freedom. It’s not just about solving problems. It’s about building possibilities.

How I Got Here

I ended up in IT almost by chance. At first, I wanted to go to art school, but I chose tech. Looking back, it was the right decision. I discovered that programming can be as creative as drawing—it just uses a different canvas.

My first website was a small video-game store. It was messy, but I loved it. Later, I learned about SQL databases and backend logic, and even tried game development. I made a Flappy Bird clone in Unity. It wasn’t great, but it taught me so much. Every line of code tells a story.
